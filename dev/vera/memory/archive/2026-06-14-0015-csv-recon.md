## The wallet-recovery pattern (16 self-Execute calls)

These are Execute calls where the bot sends a transaction *to itself*. This is the wallet-recovery bridge pattern: when the in-memory position map and the on-chain wallet diverge, the bot runs a self-Execute to bridge the gap. **16 of these in 8 days is a lot.** That's roughly 2 per active trading day.

The pattern in the data:
- 2026-06-05: 2 self-executes (early days, lots of position discovery)
- 2026-06-06: 1
- 2026-06-07: 1
- 2026-06-09: 2 (cluster — suggests a wallet-recovery bridge session)
- 2026-06-10: 3 (cluster)
- 2026-06-11: 1
- 2026-06-12: 2
- 2026-06-13: 4 (incident day — most of the day was wallet-recovery, not new trades)

**The 4 self-Execute calls on 2026-06-13 are after the morning's trading losses.** This confirms the wallet-recovery pattern was firing *during the incident*, trying to close stranded positions. The "phantom 639.54 GRT" in dex_state.json is one of these self-Execute attempts that *didn't fully reconcile* — the engine thought it had the position closed but the on-chain state diverged.

## What this tells me about the engine's design

The engine has a "wallet-recovery" mode that runs periodically. It calls Execute to itself, which on Arbitrum with the right calldata can be a no-op or a state-bridging call. **This is the engine's existing mechanism for reconciling in-memory state with on-chain state.** It exists. It runs. It just doesn't work correctly in the case where:
- A position is open in dex_state
- The wallet has *some* of the tokens (e.g., 5.9 GRT) but not all (e.g., 639.54 GRT)
- The wallet-recovery bridge can't tell the difference between "I have 5.9 GRT, the position is partially real" and "I have 5.9 GRT, the position is a phantom"

**The wallet-recovery is the third mechanism the engine has for state reconciliation, in addition to:**
- `savant.blocked` (file flag, restart gate)
- `verify_proceeds` in `close_position_internal` (3x retry, masks failure as breakeven)
- `wallet-recovery` self-Execute calls (the engine's *existing* answer to the divergence problem)

**All three are broken in different ways.** This is more evidence for the "the engine has a state-reconciliation problem" framing, not just "the close-path masks losses."
