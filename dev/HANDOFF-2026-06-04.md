- **Root cause:** Missing 32-byte signature length prefix in calldata encoding
- 0x API v2 expects: `calldata || sig_length (32 bytes) || signature (65 bytes)`
- We were doing: `calldata || signature (65 bytes)`
- One missing field caused EVERY swap to revert with panic 0x41

- Also: use API-provided `permit2.hash` field instead of computing our own

## Lessons Learned

- **Read the docs before debugging.** The 0x Permit2 guide explicitly shows the signature format. We spent 4 days debugging what the docs would have told us in 5 minutes.
- **The `hash` field is pre-computed.** The 0x API returns `permit2.hash` — use it directly instead of computing your own.
- **Signature format matters.** `calldata || sig_len (32 bytes) || sig (65 bytes)` — not just `calldata || sig`.
- **Binance/Bybit are geo-blocked in the US.** Don't include them.
- **OKX and KuCoin are the best free CEX sources.** High rate limits, broad coverage.
- **ECHO Protocol compliance is non-negotiable.** We violated Laws 1-4 throughout. This led to 4 days of wasted debugging.
