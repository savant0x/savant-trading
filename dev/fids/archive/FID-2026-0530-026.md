## Summary

First paper test revealed 7 calibration issues (5 found in test, 2 found in Perfection Loop audit). The bot completed 2 full cycles (28 decisions) but held ALL positions. The SOUL.md's conditions are too strict when combined — weekend + elevated funding + low volume gives enough disqualifiers to reject everything. These are multi-million-dollar market cap coins on a major exchange — low volume doesn't mean no opportunity. The bot needs to trade when edge exists, not just when conditions are perfect.

---

## Issues

### CAL-1. Funding rate unit misinterpreted — AI treats annualized as per-8hr

**Evidence:** Engine logs `rate=13.493704%` for BTC. Kraken Futures returns annualized rates. The SOUL.md threshold is `> 0.05% per 8hr` = overleveraged. 13.49% annualized = ~0.012%/8hr — well BELOW the threshold. But the AI is treating 13.49% as per-8hr and rejecting every trade.

**Fix:** Add explicit unit clarification to SOUL.md Section 4.5: "Kraken Futures funding rates are ANNUALIZED. Convert to per-8hr by dividing by (365 × 3). A 15% annualized rate = 0.014%/8hr = normal. A 100% annualized rate = 0.091%/8hr = elevated." Also add conversion instruction to the AI prompt context.

**Verification:** Next run should show AI correctly identifying 13.49% annualized as normal, not extreme.

**Lines changed:** ~10

### CAL-2. Zero volume on latest candle is an incomplete-candle artifact

**Evidence:** AI rejects trades citing "ZERO VOLUME on latest candle." The engine pops the last candle (incomplete), but the "latest" in context may be a low-volume period candle, not actually zero volume.

**Fix:** In `context_builder.rs`, add note to user message: "Note: Latest candle volume may appear low if candle is still forming. Do not use latest-candle volume as a disqualifier — use 20-period volume SMA instead."

**Verification:** AI should stop citing "zero volume" as a primary disqualifier.

**Lines changed:** ~5

### CAL-3. Weekend is a size reducer, not a disqualifier

**Evidence:** AI cites "Weekend session with 0.7x size multiplier" as a reason to HOLD. The SOUL.md says "Weekend: Lower volume but still tradeable. Reduced size." The AI is treating 0.7x as "don't trade" instead of "trade smaller."

**Fix:** Update SOUL.md Section 4.1 to be explicit: "Weekend sessions reduce position size by 30% (0.7x multiplier) but do NOT disqualify trades. If a setup exists with edge, take it at reduced size. Weekend is a sizing parameter, not a veto."

**Verification:** AI should take weekend trades at 0.7x size when setup exists.

**Lines changed:** ~5

### CAL-4. LOW conviction should trade at 0.5%, not HOLD

**Evidence:** The SOUL.md conviction table says `NONE = 0% — do not trade`. But the AI is classifying setups with 1-2 aligned factors as "NONE" instead of "LOW." With weekend + slightly elevated funding + low volume, the AI has enough to classify as NONE even when there's a valid technical setup.

**Fix:** Update SOUL.md conviction table to be explicit: "LOW = 1 factor aligned, others neutral → 0.5% risk. Only NONE when factors actively CONFLICT (bullish technical + bearish funding). If factors are simply neutral (not conflicting), that is LOW conviction, not NONE."

**Verification:** AI should take LOW conviction trades at 0.5% risk instead of HOLDing.

**Lines changed:** ~10

### CAL-5. These are multi-million-dollar market cap coins — volume is always sufficient

**Evidence:** AI cites "zero volume" and "low liquidity" for BTC, ETH, SOL, XRP — coins with billions in daily volume. The "low volume" is relative to their peak, not absolute.

**Fix:** Add to SOUL.md Section 4.1: "The 15 pairs on Kraken are all top-50 market cap coins with >$100M daily volume. 'Low volume' on these pairs means slightly below average, NOT illiquid. Do not disqualify top-50 coins for volume reasons — the liquidity is always sufficient for a $50 account."

**Verification:** AI should stop citing volume as a disqualifier for major cap coins.

**Lines changed:** ~5

### CAL-6. Summary displays funding rate without unit label

**Evidence:** `aggregator.rs:74-76` formats as `Funding Rate: {:.4}%`. AI sees "Funding Rate: 13.4937%" with no indication of whether this is per-8hr or annualized. The `FundingData` struct stores the per-8hr rate in `funding_rate` and the annualized rate in `funding_rate_annualized`, but neither field is labeled.

**Fix:** Change `summary()` format to `Funding Rate: {:.4}% (per-8hr)` so the AI knows the unit. Also add annualized display: `Annualized: {:.2}%`.

**Verification:** AI context should show "Funding Rate: 0.0123% (per-8hr) | Annualized: 13.49%"

**Lines changed:** ~5

### CAL-7. Annualized funding rate calculated but never displayed

**Evidence:** `funding_rates.rs:156` calculates `funding_rate_annualized = Some(fr * 3.0 * 365.0)` but this field is never read by `summary()` or `context_builder.rs`. The AI has no way to see the annualized perspective.

**Fix:** Add `funding_rate_annualized` display to `summary()` after the per-8hr rate. This gives the AI both perspectives: "0.0123%/8hr (13.49% annualized)".

**Verification:** AI should see both rates and correctly identify 13.49% annualized as normal.

**Lines changed:** ~5
