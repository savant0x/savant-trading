## What Is Day Trading?

Day trading is getting in and out of trades — stocks, crypto, futures, or other instruments — within a short time frame. Unlike traditional investing where you buy and hold for years, day traders typically do not hold positions longer than one day.

**Trade durations can range from:**

- 1 minute
- 5–10 minutes
- 1–2 hours
- Same day (never overnight)

**Advantages of day trading over long-term investing:**

- **Low capital requirement** — you don't need a lot of money to start
- **Multiple trades per day** — allows compounding and faster account growth
- **More control** — active decision-making rather than hoping a stock goes up over decades

**Markets available for day trading:**

| Market | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Stock Market** | Buying and selling individual stocks |
| **Options** | Contracts based on underlying stock price movement |
| **Futures** | Contracts on commodities, indices, and more *(primary focus of this guide)* |
| **Crypto** | Bitcoin, Ethereum, and other digital assets |
| **Forex** | Foreign exchange currency pairs |

> **Key Insight:** The principles in this guide apply across all markets, but the strategy is specifically demonstrated using futures trading — which is the recommended starting point for complete beginners.

## Understanding Candlesticks

### What Are Candlesticks?

Candlesticks represent price movement during a specific time period. Each candlestick tells a story: where price started, where it ended, and where it traveled in between.

### Candlestick Components

| Component | Definition |
|-----------|------------|
| **Body** | The thick part of the candlestick |
| **Wick** | The thin lines extending above and below the body |

### Bullish (Green) Candlesticks

- **Bottom of the body** = where price **opened** (start of the time period)
- **Top of the body** = where price **closed** (end of the time period)
- **Top of the wick** = the **highest** price reached during that period
- **Bottom of the wick** = the **lowest** price reached during that period

### Bearish (Red) Candlesticks

- **Top of the body** = where price **opened**
- **Bottom of the body** = where price **closed**
- **Top of the wick** = the **highest** price reached
- **Bottom of the wick** = the **lowest** price reached

### How Time Frames Work

Each candlestick represents the price action that occurred during its time frame period:

- On the **1-minute chart**, each candle = 1 minute of price action
- On the **1-hour chart**, each candle = 1 hour of price action
- On the **4-hour chart**, each candle = 4 hours of price action
- On the **daily chart**, each candle = 1 full trading day

> **Key Insight:** Every candlestick is simply telling a story of what price did during that time period. Trading is about reading patterns in these stories to predict what price might do next.

## Heikin Ashi Candlesticks

Heikin Ashi candlesticks are a modified candlestick type used in the strategy covered in this guide.

**How they differ from regular candlesticks:**

- The body of each Heikin Ashi candle starts at the **middle of the previous candle's body** (not at the previous close)
- The top of a bullish (green) Heikin Ashi candle represents the **average** of where price has been during that period — not the closing price
- The bottom of a bearish (red) Heikin Ashi candle similarly represents the average price

**Wicks still work the same way** — they show where price traveled throughout the candlestick period.

> **Key Insight:** Heikin Ashi candles smooth out price action and make trends easier to identify visually. You don't need to deeply understand the math behind them to use the strategy — just know that they are required for this setup.

## Support & Resistance

Support and resistance are foundational concepts every trader must understand.

### The Concept

- **Resistance** = a ceiling — a price level where selling pressure tends to push price back down
- **Support** = a floor — a price level where buying pressure tends to push price back up

**Example:** If gold repeatedly sells off at $1,000 (resistance) and bounces at $500 (support), the market has established a range. Traders recognize these levels and act on them, reinforcing the pattern.

### Why It Matters

Price tends to **reject from the same areas** repeatedly because the collective market considers those prices "too expensive" (resistance) or "too cheap" (support). Recognizing these zones helps you:

- Avoid entering trades at unfavorable price levels
- Anticipate potential reversals
- Identify high-probability areas for entries and exits

> **Key Insight:** Price will most of the time reject at the same areas it previously rejected at. Understanding support and resistance zones is essential before trading any strategy.

## The Golden Rule: Stick To One Strategy

> **Key Principle:** Find ONE strategy and stick to it. Do not hop from strategy to strategy.

This is the single most important lesson from the speaker's trading journey. After years of switching strategies and staying unprofitable, mastering one strategy is what finally produced consistent results.

**Why traders fail by switching strategies:**

1. They see a video claiming a "100% win rate" strategy
2. They just had a losing trade on their current strategy
3. They switch to the new strategy, abandoning months or years of work
4. The new strategy also has losses, so they switch again
5. This cycle repeats indefinitely, keeping them permanently unprofitable

> **Key Insight:** The majority of strategies work when paired with proper risk management. The failure point is usually that traders skip steps in the checklist or abandon proper risk management — not that the strategy itself is broken.

**The real cause of failure:**

- A strategy has 10 required steps — a trader only follows 8, then blames the strategy
- A trader has a few losing days and assumes the strategy is broken
- A trader sees someone else's results and assumes a different strategy is superior

> **Key Principle:** Strategy hopping is the biggest trap that will keep you unprofitable far longer than necessary. Mastering one strategy can make you profitable within weeks or months — not years.

## Step 1: Chart Setup

**Required settings:**

1. **Candlestick type:** Switch to **Heikin Ashi** candlesticks (dropdown menu on TradingView)
2. **Time frame:** **1-minute chart**
3. **Indicator:** Add the **100 EMA** (Exponential Moving Average)

**How to add the 100 EMA on TradingView:**

1. Click the **Indicators** button
2. Type **"EMA"** in the search bar
3. Select **"Moving Average Exponential"**
4. Click the **settings/gear icon** on the EMA label
5. Go to the **Inputs** tab
6. Change the **Length** from 9 to **100**
7. (Optional) Go to the **Style** tab and change the line color to white for visibility on a dark background
8. Click **OK**

## Step 2: Market Structure (EMA Direction)

The 100 EMA determines which direction you trade:

| Condition | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Candlesticks are **above** the EMA | Only look for **buy** (long) trades |
| Candlesticks are **below** the EMA | Only look for **sell** (short) trades |

**Logic:** The EMA shows the general trend direction. If price is above the EMA, the market is trending up — only buy. If price is below the EMA, the market is trending down — only sell. Always trade in the direction of the trend.

> **Key Principle:** Always be on the same side as the market. The EMA tells you which side that is.

---

## Step 3: Clean Pullback

After identifying the trend direction, wait for a **pullback** — a temporary move against the trend.

**Why pullbacks matter:** Price never moves in a straight line. In an uptrend, price goes up, pulls back slightly, then continues up. In a downtrend, price goes down, pulls back slightly, then continues down. The strategy capitalizes on the end of these pullbacks.

**Definition of a clean pullback:**

- At least **two clean opposite-color candlesticks** moving in the pullback direction
- "Clean" means **no wicks on the side facing the trend:**
  - For **buy setups** (pullback is bearish): look for bearish candles with **no wicks on top**
  - For **sell setups** (pullback is bullish): look for bullish candles with **no wicks on bottom**

> **Key Insight:** A clean pullback shows that the counter-trend move has conviction. Two or more clean candles in the pullback direction confirm the pullback is real, setting up the reversal entry.

---

## Step 4: High-Volume Doji Entry

After a clean pullback, wait for a **high-volume doji candlestick** as the entry signal.

### What Is a Doji?

A doji candlestick has:
- A **small body** (open and close are nearly equal)
- **Long wicks** on both top and bottom

It signals **indecision** in the market — price went up and down but ended where it started. After a pullback, indecision often precedes a reversal back in the trend direction.

### High-Volume vs. Low-Volume Doji

| Doji Type | Definition | Action |
|-----------|------------|--------|
| **High-volume** | The doji (body + wicks) is **larger than the candlestick(s) before it** | **Enter the trade** |
| **Low-volume** | The doji is the **same size or smaller** than the preceding candlestick(s) | **Skip the trade** |

> **Key Principle:** Only enter on high-volume doji candlesticks. If the doji is small relative to the candles before it, it does not have enough volume to signal a valid reversal. Never skip this rule — even if the trade would have worked out, discipline matters more than individual results.

**Entry timing:** Enter the trade as soon as the doji candlestick closes.

---

## Step 5: Stop Loss Placement

| Trade Direction | Stop Loss Placement |
|----------------|---------------------|
| **Buy (long)** | Below the low of the doji candlestick you entered on |
| **Sell (short)** | Above the high of the doji candlestick you entered on |

The stop loss automatically exits the trade if price moves against you beyond the doji, limiting your loss to a defined amount.

---

## Step 6: Take Profit — 1:1 Risk-to-Reward

Set your take profit at a **1:1 risk-to-reward ratio**:

- If your stop loss risks **$100**, your take profit target is **$100 in profit**
- Measured by dragging the take-profit level on TradingView's "long position" or "short position" tool until the risk/reward ratio reads **1:1**

**For beginners:** Stick strictly to 1:1 risk-to-reward. More advanced techniques (holding for 2:1 or 3:1) can be learned later.

> **Key Insight:** At a 1:1 risk-to-reward ratio with a 60%+ win rate, you are consistently profitable. The math works in your favor over a large sample of trades.

## Prop Firms & Funded Accounts

### What Is a Prop Firm?

A **proprietary trading firm** (prop firm) is a company that funds traders with their capital. You pay a small fee to access a large account, trade their money, and keep a percentage of the profits.

### How Funded Accounts Work

| Step | Description |
|------|-------------|
| 1. Pay a fee | Typically $200–$600 depending on account size |
| 2. Receive funding | Access to a $25K, $50K, $100K, or $150K trading account |
| 3. Trade the account | Follow the firm's rules and use their capital |
| 4. Keep profits | Retain 80–90% of what you earn |
| 5. Loss protection | If you lose money, you owe nothing beyond the initial fee |

### Why Prop Firms Change Everything for Beginners

> **Key Insight:** Instead of risking $20,000 of your own money (as the speaker did early on), you can pay $500 and trade a $100,000 account. The maximum you can ever lose is that initial $500 fee — never more.

**Example:**

- Pay $500 → access a $100,000 account
- Make $10,000 using the strategy → withdraw $8,000–$9,000 (80–90% profit split)
- If you lose $4,000 on the account → you owe nothing; only the initial $500 is lost

### Warning: Not All Prop Firms Are Equal

Some prop firms have:
- Difficult "challenge" phases designed to make you fail
- Rules that are intentionally restrictive to prevent payouts
- Business models that profit from traders repeatedly buying accounts

**What to look for in a good prop firm:**

- No challenge phases (immediate access to trade)
- Trader-friendly rules with reasonable drawdown limits
- High profit split (80–90%)
- Transparent and reliable payout history

> **Key Principle:** Research any prop firm thoroughly before paying. Look for firms with trader-friendly rules, no challenge phases, and transparent payout histories.

## Practice & Journaling

### Practice

> **Key Principle:** Practice is the most important part of trading. Memorize the checklist, look at charts every single day, and put in as many hours as possible.

**What to practice:**

- Identifying Heikin Ashi candlestick patterns
- Spotting clean pullbacks
- Recognizing high-volume vs. low-volume doji candlesticks
- Placing stop losses and take profits correctly
- Walking through historical charts to find setups

### Journaling

Journal every single trade. This creates the data you need to identify patterns in your own behavior and improve over time.

**What to record for each trade:**

- What you traded (NQ, crypto, etc.)
- Time of entry
- Direction (buy or sell)
- Win or loss
- Strategy used
- Why you think you won or lost

> **Key Insight:** Trading is about patterns — both in price and in your own behavior. After 100 trades, your journal will reveal exactly which mistakes are costing you money, allowing you to fix them systematically.

## The Importance of Community

Trading is a solitary activity — sitting in front of charts for hours, making decisions alone. This isolation is one of the biggest challenges traders face.

**Why community matters:**

- Most people outside trading (friends, family, classmates) won't understand what you do
- Having someone to discuss trades with, share ideas, and stay accountable accelerates growth
- Being surrounded by like-minded people at various stages (beginners, profitable traders, six-figure traders) provides both motivation and education

**Options for finding community:**

- A friend who trades alongside you
- A free Discord or trading community
- A paid mentorship group with live trading and coaching

> **Key Insight:** The journey to profitability can get lonely. Having even one other person who understands trading makes a significant difference in staying motivated and disciplined.
