## Market Context — Where We Are Now

Since October–November, markets have been brutal. The expectation was that Bitcoin breaking its all-time high would trigger the typical pattern: alts begin to run, meme coins go parabolic, and money starts flowing. This time was different. When Bitcoin broke its all-time high towards the end of last year, crypto essentially topped out. Alts ran briefly, but nothing like previous cycles.

The main thesis at the time was built on **interest rate cuts**. Historically, Bitcoin and alts really begin to move following rate cuts — one of the key reasons the 2021 cycle was so strong. But when that thesis got invalidated, risk-on assets began to unravel. Crypto Twitter sentiment turned bearish, people started capitulating, and most positions went into the red.

> **Key Insight:** Unlike the stock market, crypto is driven primarily by **speculation and hype**. Stocks have earnings and real company performance. Gold is tangible with finite supply. Crypto, because of its permissionless and unauditable nature, is a fully irrational market — and that's both the risk and the reward.

## Understanding Crypto Markets — The Double-Edged Sword

Crypto is the only market where you experience true high risk and high reward. That reward does not come without risk. This is the wild west — things can always go to zero. But the conviction is that **crypto is never going to disappear**. Bitcoin will eventually be a million dollars in our lifetime, and altcoins will continue making new floors as cycles progress.

The stock market has been around for **400 years**. Cryptocurrency has only existed since Bitcoin's launch in January 2009 — roughly **16 years old**. When you compare a 400-year-old market to a 16-year-old market, massive fluctuations are expected. Combine that with permissionless systems and a trust-based market, and volatility is the price of admission.

> **Key Insight:** It's incredibly foolish to treat crypto like the same type of market as stocks or something more conservative. Crypto offers exponential returns precisely because of the risk involved — and the edge comes from identifying early trends and narratives before the crowd.

With **Powell leaving office May 15** and **Kevin Warsh** taking over, the transition itself adds uncertainty — but also potential for policy shifts.

### 2. Midterm Elections (November 2026)

Since the 1950s, the average one-year return following midterms has been **15.4%** — twice the return of all other years. Three reasons:

1. **Policy certainty** — Once the election is over, the legislative agenda locks in for two years, removing uncertainty
2. **Gridlock** — The president's party often loses seats, creating gridlock. Gridlock prevents surprise legislation, which builds market confidence
3. **Economic front-loading** — Presidents front-load pain in years one and two, then juice the economy for years three and four to improve re-election odds. Trump follows this pattern

### 3. War Developments

The Iran situation is being underestimated. War announcements cause massive market dips — and could be the catalyst that pushes Bitcoin under $50,000. Each dip is an opportunity, but only if you're prepared with a plan.

## How to Invest in 2026 — The Playbook

### Core Strategy: Dollar-Cost Averaging

Don't try to time the exact bottom. Millions of market participants will be trying to do the same thing. The best course of action for 90% of people:

1. **Know your numbers** — Understand exactly how much is leaving your wallet and how much is coming in
2. **Cut unnecessary spending** — Eliminate reckless expenses
3. **DCA consistently** — Accumulate Bitcoin during the bear market window
4. **Have firepower ready** — Keep dry powder for the real buying opportunities

> **Key Insight:** Don't buy dips mindlessly. Buying the dip only works in an **uptrending market structure**. In a downtrend, the blood can bleed out more. Know the difference between time frames and risk tolerance.

### Risk Management Rules

- **You're not going to hit everything** — Nobody in the world can bat 100%
- **Size up in early narratives** — Don't go completely degen
- **Bigger winners make up for losers** — A few true home runs make your losses a small fraction of overall portfolio growth
- **Manage risk the hard way** — Learning from nearly losing everything after the 2021 cycle taught the importance of position sizing

Despite widespread skepticism, NFTs were successful in their first wave — they achieved massive adoption, attracted celebrities, and proved the concept. With companies like **Meta** and **Oakley** creating hardware that converges the real and digital worlds, there is a legitimate place for NFTs. Expect a resurgence in the **latter half of 2027 going into 2028** — hopefully one that sticks this time.
