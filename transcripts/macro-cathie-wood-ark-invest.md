## Introduction — Who Is Cathie Wood?

Cathie Wood is the CEO and Chief Investment Officer of ARK Invest, overseeing nearly **$30 billion** in assets. She is widely regarded as one of the most influential investors in disruptive innovation, having built a multi-billion dollar fund by identifying transformative technology trends before mainstream markets.

Her investment focus spans five major innovation platforms: **robotics, energy storage, artificial intelligence, blockchain technology, and multiomic sequencing** in the life sciences. With over 40 years of market experience — starting at age 20 at Capital Group, introduced by economist **Art Laffer** — Wood has become one of the most prominent voices in growth investing.

> **Key Insight:** "Invest in companies that are technologically enabled and that are going to transform the world as we know it."

## Tesla — The Largest AI Project on Earth

ARK's largest position in its flagship **ARKK** strategy is Tesla. Wood has been bullish on Tesla since **2015**, when she predicted the stock (pre-split) would exceed $4,000 — a call that proved correct by a significant margin, with the stock reaching the equivalent of roughly $12,000–$18,000 at its peak.

### The Robotaxi Thesis

**Cathie:** Tesla is the largest AI project on Earth. We believe the entire ecosystem associated with autonomous taxi networks is going to be worth **$8 to $10 trillion** in terms of revenue generation in the next 5 to 10 years. To put that in context, the entire GDP of the world today is about $130 trillion.

Tesla's robotaxi strategy has two phases:

1. **Austin launch (June 2025):** Model Y vehicles operating as autonomous taxis using Full Self-Driving (FSD)
2. **Cyber Cab launch (2026):** Purpose-built autonomous taxi vehicles

The economics are transformative. Today, an Uber costs roughly **$2–4 per mile**. ARK believes Tesla will be able to offer autonomous taxi service for **$25 cents per mile** at scale.

> **Key Insight:** "If I chose to, I could have my Model Y drive me to work and then let it out for the day and earn money on it, have it pick me up at the end of the night."

### Tesla Stock Price Target

| Metric | Current | ARK 5-Year Target |
|--------|---------|-------------------|
| **Stock Price** | ~$270–280 | **$2,600** |
| **Valuation Driver** | EV sales | **90% from robotaxi platform** |
| **Humanoid Robots** | Minimal | Not included in $2,600 target |

The electric car is a one-shot sale. The robotaxi platform creates a **recurring revenue model** — either subscription-based or à la carte — where every Tesla on the road can potentially become an income-generating asset.

### The eVTOL Extension

The dramatic cost reduction in ground transportation led ARK to study **eVTOLs** (electric vertical takeoff and landing vehicles). When you cut the price of something, demand increases — and that increased congestion on roads drives demand for air mobility.

ARK holds **Archer** in its portfolio, an eVTOL company that also signed an exclusive deal with **Anduril**, the most sophisticated defense technology company, for autonomous drone applications.

## Healthcare Revolution — Cancer Detection and Gene Editing

**Cathie:** We think the most profound application of AI is going to be in healthcare.

### Early Cancer Detection

Because of AI, it is now possible to **diagnose cancer with a blood test in stage one**. If you discover cancer in stage one, you can save most people — and potentially even detect it before stage one.

This capability comes from the **convergence of three technologies:**

1. **DNA sequencing**
2. **RNA and protein sequencing**
3. **CRISPR gene editing**

### CRISPR Therapeutics

**CRISPR Therapeutics**, one of the largest positions in ARKG and a top-10 holding in ARKK, has developed a therapy to **cure sickle cell disease and beta thalassemia** with a single treatment.

Both diseases are already generating revenue for CRISPR Therapeutics and **Vertex Pharmaceuticals**. The preconditioning regimen is currently intensive (similar to chemotherapy), but the demand is enormous — patients with these conditions visit the emergency room 10–20 times per year for blood transfusions.

> **Key Insight:** The convergence of sequencing technologies and CRISPR gene editing is beginning to cure disease — not just manage it, but cure it.

## Bitcoin — From $250 to a $1.5 Million Forecast

ARK first invested in Bitcoin in the **summer of 2015** at roughly **$250** per coin. At the time of the interview, Bitcoin was trading around **$104,000**.

### The Origin Story

ARK's first Bitcoin analyst, **Chris Bradysky**, wrote a white paper exploring whether Bitcoin could serve the three roles of money: **means of exchange**, **store of value**, and **unit of account**. Art Laffer reviewed the paper and told Wood:

> "This is what I've been waiting for since the US closed the gold window in 1971. A rules-based global monetary system like Bretton Woods under the gold exchange standard."

When Wood asked how big the idea was, Laffer pointed out that the US monetary base was $4.5 trillion, while Bitcoin's network value was only **$6 billion**. Wood bought Bitcoin personally at $250 because regulators wouldn't allow it in the fund at the time.

### Path to Institutional Adoption

The SEC approved the **spot Bitcoin ETF in January 2024**, opening the door for institutional investment. Institutions are only now beginning to commit, and there is a supply constraint:

| Bitcoin Metric | Value |
|---------------|-------|
| **Current Supply** | ~20 million BTC mined |
| **Maximum Supply** | 21 million BTC |
| **Remaining to Mine** | ~1 million BTC (~$100B at current prices) |
| **Current Market Cap** | ~$2 trillion |
| **ARK 2030 Price Target** | **$1.5 million per BTC** |

### The Three Building Blocks of the $1.5M Forecast

1. **Institutional Investment** — Has barely started. Institutions managing trillions must now consider Bitcoin as competitors adopt it.
2. **Digital Gold** — Young people are far more comfortable with digital gold than physical gold as a store of value.
3. **Emerging Market Insurance** — Bitcoin and stablecoins provide a critical hedge for citizens in countries with undisciplined fiscal and monetary policy (e.g., Venezuela).

> **Key Insight:** "Bitcoin is a new asset class. We haven't had one truly since equities in the 1600s." Bitcoin's base layer (Layer 1) has not been hacked since its release in 2009 — an extraordinary security record.

### Why Bitcoin Over Other Digital Assets

| Attribute | Bitcoin | Ethereum / Solana |

| **Global Monetary System** | Yes | No |
| **New Asset Class** | Largest | Part of, but smaller |

## AI, Jobs, and the Future of Work

### Vibe Coding

A new paradigm called **vibe coding** is emerging, enabled by natural language programming. Using tools like ChatGPT, Codex, Replit, and Anthropic's models, anyone who speaks a natural language can describe what they want to build — and AI generates the code.

**Cathie:** We're going to replace some of our software that we're buying from outsiders and customize it because we don't have to buy off the shelf anymore. One size fits all is over. There's going to be a lot more customization, personalization, and a creativity explosion.

### Demographics and Productivity

The developed world faces a **population collapse** — falling fertility rates in China, Japan, Europe, and the US. Baby boomers are retiring, and the generations following them are smaller.

> **Key Insight:** "Productivity is going to be essential. If we can't find human beings, we're going to need productivity to fill the gap."

This demographic reality makes AI and robotics not threats to employment, but **necessities** for maintaining economic output.

### Will Unemployment Rise?

**Cathie:** In 5 or 10 years, assuming no policy mistake and no recession, I think unemployment will be the same or lower. Most of this is because baby boomers are retiring and coming out of the labor force, and the generations following them are smaller.

### The AlphaGo Lesson

When Google's AlphaGo defeated the world Go champion, the player was initially crestfallen — then began **training against machines**. His game improved so dramatically that when he competed against other humans, he dominated.

> **Key Insight:** "People don't want to see machines competing against machines. But in the world of work, the incentive is productivity. My humanoid robot isn't going to get sick, and it's going to have a PhD in everything."

### Who Gets Left Behind?

Wood expressed concern about people in **disrupted industries** who are unwilling or unable to adapt — particularly those outside major urban centers who may not encounter these technologies through their work or daily life.

**Cathie:** There's going to be so much opportunity. It's going to be so exciting. But initiative is the key word. For those who are healthy and willing to learn and dream and use their imaginations — they'll find it.

## ARK's Investment Philosophy — Wright's Law and Convergence

### Three Characteristics of an Innovation Platform

1. **Wright's Law** — The most important criterion. Technology is deflationary; costs fall over time and are passed through as lower prices or better performance. Wright's Law measures the learning curve and how fast costs decline.
2. **Cross-Sector Proliferation** — The technologies cut across economic sectors and apply to more than one group of people.
3. **Launching Pads** — Each technology serves as a foundation for new technologies. DNA sequencing was required before CRISPR gene editing could exist.

### Can AI Replace ARK's Research?

**Cathie:** AI can use pattern recognition based on history to maybe do what we're doing. But we're doing original research on a white sheet of paper — there's no history for this. Disruptive means the traditional world order and patterns that robots would recognize is going to change.

## Trump, Tariffs, and Deregulation

When Trump was elected, the stock market surged. The promise was **deregulation, lower taxes, and lower interest rates**.

**Cathie:** The deregulation piece is underestimated in terms of how important it is, because we're strangling in regulation. This is not our DNA. We got to get out from under this.

However, the process felt chaotic. Tariff escalation — including the threat of massive tariffs on countries like Canada (which charged a 250% tariff on US milk) — paralyzed businesses and initially scared investors.

### What Changed

The turning point came when **Treasury Secretary Bessent** pushed aside **Peter Navarro** on tariff policy, signaling a more pragmatic approach.

**Cathie:** Throughout all of this chaos, what Trump is trying to do is not only get tariffs down throughout the world, but more importantly, get **non-tariff trade barriers** down. He also knows that he has to sound crazy for other people to take him seriously.

> **Key Insight:** "I do think it's going to work out. And I think the stock market is beginning to smell it."

## Elon Musk — The Thomas Edison of Our Age

**Cathie:** I think he's the Thomas Edison of our age in terms of his innovative ingenuity. I also think, having met him a number of times, he's a very good person. He wants to do the right thing to transform the lot of most of humanity.

### Management Style

Musk attracts the best scientists, engineers, and business people — those who want to solve the hardest problems in the world. His approach:

1. Set a strategy and reasonable milestone timelines
2. Don't interfere unless milestones are missed
3. If milestones are missed, get deeply involved — fire and restructure as needed
4. Once the program is back on track, step back

**Cathie:** He's really the troubleshooter-in-chief.

### DOGE and Government Efficiency

Musk's involvement with the Department of Government Efficiency (DOGE) impacted Tesla's Q1 performance — both through the political backlash (decline in vehicle sales) and the macroeconomic environment (negative real GDP growth in Q1).

**Cathie:** Our government has become so bloated, it is scary. Government spending is taxation — either immediate taxation, future taxation, or inflation, which is the most regressive tax of all. I'm watching how they're starting to use AI in the FDA — it's phenomenal what's happening.

Musk announced he would step away from DOGE to spend more time with his companies.

## Cathie Wood's Career and the Founding of ARK

### Early Career and Art Laffer

Wood began investing at age 20 at **Capital Group**, introduced by economist **Art Laffer** — creator of the Laffer Curve and advisor to presidents from Nixon through Trump.

**Cathie:** My mission when I started was to make my boss look brilliant. I figured out how to make charts on the ancient time-sharing mainframe system, and I delighted my boss. I loved learning about technology and economics through him.

Laffer's mentorship gave Wood a deep foundation in macroeconomics and the confidence that came with it.

> **Key Insight:** "When I walked into the financial world, I knew more about economics than most of the people in the room. That was a great source of confidence."

### The Laffer Letter

Art Laffer wrote a letter describing Wood:

> *"There was this young lady named Cathy Duddy, later Cathy Wood, whose face was the map of Ireland and whose ambition was over the moon. I was a tough teacher and grader, and Cathy's first steps were shaky, but in short order she rose to the occasion and aced the course."*

Wood gave Laffer **1% of ARK Invest** when she founded the company in 2014. Now 84–85 years old, Laffer has been rejuvenated by Bitcoin — recently starting an X account (despite having a flip phone and no email) to spread the word about digital assets and stablecoins.

### Advice for Young People

**Cathie:** My advice to all young people getting into their first job: make your boss look brilliant. If you do, you owe them a debt of gratitude if they give you growth opportunities. If they don't, then it's time to go to the next place where you make that next boss look brilliant.
